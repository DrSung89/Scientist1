Molarity (M) is the most common unit of concentration used in chemistry and biology. It is defined as the number of moles of solute dissolved in one liter of solution. Accurate molarity calculations are crucial for preparing buffers, media, and reagents in the laboratory.

The Formula

The basic formula for calculating molarity is:

$$ M = \frac{n}{V} $$
Where:
  • M = Molarity (mol/L)
  • n = Moles of solute (mass / molecular weight)
  • V = Volume of solution (L)

How to Use This Calculator

This tool allows you to calculate any of the four variables by inputting the other three. It is particularly useful for:

  • Making Solutions: Calculate how much powder (mass) is needed to make a specific volume of a known concentration.
  • Dilutions: Verify the concentration of a solution prepared with a specific mass and volume.

Example Calculation

Suppose you want to prepare 500 mL of 1 M NaCl solution. The molecular weight (MW) of NaCl is approx. 58.44 g/mol.

  1. Enter 1 in Concentration (M).
  2. Enter 500 in Volume and select (mL).
  3. Enter 58.44 in Molecular Weight.
  4. Click Calculate. The result will show that you need 29.22 g of NaCl.
