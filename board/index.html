handshake Community Guidelines & Purpose

The Scientist's Toolkit Community Board is designed to foster collaboration and knowledge-sharing among researchers, graduate students, and principal investigators globally. Science thrives on open communication, whether you are troubleshooting a failed Western blot, selecting the right statistical model (such as Welch's T-Test vs. Student's T-Test), or requesting a new feature for our web calculators.

What to Post Here:

  • Statistical Methodology Questions: Not sure if your data requires an IQR outlier removal method or a Grubb's test? Ask the community for guidance.
  • Calculator Feature Requests: If your research requires a specific equation (e.g., specific pediatric HED formulas or one-way ANOVA), let us know so we can build it.
  • Bug Reports: Found an unexpected P-value or a UI glitch? Please report it here with the numbers you inputted so we can quickly patch the system.
  • Protocol Sharing: Share your optimized buffer recipes, molarity tips, or assay troubleshooting techniques.

warning Privacy & Data Protection Warning

Please remember that this is a public forum. Do not post sensitive, unpublished raw data, proprietary chemical structures, or patient-identifiable information (PII). If you are asking a statistical question, please use dummy variables or generalized descriptions (e.g., "Group A vs Group B") to protect the integrity of your upcoming publications.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do I need an account to post?

No. The Scientist's Toolkit board operates anonymously to reduce friction. You simply need to provide a nickname and a secure password. The password is required solely so that you can edit or delete your own post later if you make a mistake.

Who answers the questions posted here?

Questions are answered by the community, which includes peer researchers and occasionally the Ph.D. developer of the Scientist's Toolkit. While we strive for accuracy, please verify all critical statistical advice with your institution's principal investigator or biostatistician before manuscript submission.
