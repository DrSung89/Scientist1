üìä Outlier Detection: IQR & Normality

Generally, outliers are detected using Z-scores, which assumes the data follows a normal distribution. However, biological data is often skewed. Therefore, we provide both a Normality Test and the IQR Method.

üìâ Normality Test (Shapiro-Wilk):
To determine if data is normally distributed (P-value), this tool uses Royston's Approximation Algorithm (1992). This method allows for the accurate calculation of the Shapiro-Wilk W statistic and its significance level (P-value) directly in the browser without requiring extensive statistical tables.
IQR Calculation Formula:
1. Calculate IQR = Q3 (75th percentile) - Q1 (25th percentile)
2. Define Fences (Thresholds):
    ‚Ä¢ Lower Limit: Q1 - 1.5 √ó IQR
    ‚Ä¢ Upper Limit: Q3 + 1.5 √ó IQR

*Data points outside these limits are outliers if P < 0.05.

üêÅ Human Equivalent Dose (HED)

This calculator converts doses between species based on Body Surface Area (BSA) normalization.

Planning Animal Studies from Human Doses:
When calculating a starting dose for animal experiments based on a human approved dose (e.g., a flat dose in mg), you must first convert it to mg/kg.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Note:
For this conversion, use the FDA standard human body weight of 60 kg. Do not use arbitrary weights unless specific data is available.

Step 1 (Human Dose to mg/kg):
Human Dose (mg/kg) = Total Human Dose (mg) / 60 kg

Step 2 (Conversion to Animal Dose):
Animal Dose (mg/kg) = Human Dose (mg/kg) √ó (Human Km / Animal Km)
