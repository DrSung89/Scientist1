ğŸ² Chi-Square Test for Independence

The Chi-Square (Ï‡Â²) test of independence is used to determine if there is a significant relationship between two categorical variables. Unlike the T-test which handles numerical data (means), the Chi-square test handles frequencies (counts).

When to use this tool?

Use this calculator when you have count data usually organized in a 2x2 contingency table. Common examples in research include:

  • Genetics: Comparing observed genotype frequencies against expected Mendelian ratios.
  • Clinical Studies: Comparing "Cured vs. Not Cured" counts in Treatment vs. Control groups.

The Formula

$$ \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O_i - E_i)^2}{E_i} $$

Where:

  • O = Observed frequency
  • E = Expected frequency
