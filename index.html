Why Statistical Rigor Matters in Research

In biological research, especially in fields like Cancer Biology and pharmacology, reproducibility is the cornerstone of scientific advancement. "Statistical significance" is not just a requirement for publication; it is the mathematical validation that your experimental results are not due to random chance.

When to Use Which Calculator?

Choosing the right statistical test is crucial for data integrity. Here is a guide on when to apply each tool provided on this platform:

1. Experimental Preparation

  • Molarity & Dilution: Use these before starting any experiment. Accurate reagent preparation prevents batch effects and variability.
  • HED (Human Equivalent Dose): Essential for in vivo translational studies. Before moving from mice to humans (or vice versa), use BSA-based normalization, not just weight-based scaling.

2. Data Cleaning

  • Outlier Detector: Biological data is noisy. Use the Grubb's Test (for normal distributions) or IQR Method (for skewed data) to scientifically identify and remove anomalies without bias (p-hacking).

3. Hypothesis Testing

  • T-Test: Use when comparing the means of two groups (e.g., Control vs. Drug A). Our tool defaults to Welch's T-Test to account for unequal variances, which is safer for biological data.
  • Chi-Square Test: Use for categorical data (e.g., "Dead/Alive", "Cured/Not Cured"). It checks if the observed distribution differs from expectations.
  • Survival Analysis (Kaplan-Meier): The gold standard for time-to-event studies (e.g., measuring how long mice survive after tumor implantation). It handles "censored" data (subjects that leave the study early) properly.

Understanding P-Values and Asterisk Notation (*)

The P-value (Probability value) measures the evidence against the null hypothesis. A lower P-value means stronger evidence that your result is real. In scientific journals, statistical significance is often denoted by asterisks.
