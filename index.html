üß™ Molarity Calculator

This tool assists researchers in preparing solutions by calculating the required Mass, Concentration, Volume, or Molecular Weight (MW). Based on the fundamental formula:

Mass (g) = Concentration (mol/L) √ó Volume (L) √ó MW (g/mol)

Simply enter three known values, and the calculator will determine the fourth. It supports automatic unit conversion (e.g., mg, ¬µM, mL) to minimize manual calculation errors during experiments.

üìä Outlier Checker & Normality

This statistical tool identifies potential outliers in your experimental data using the Interquartile Range (IQR) method. It defines outliers as data points falling below Q1 - 1.5*IQR or above Q3 + 1.5*IQR.

Additionally, it performs a basic normality check using Skewness and Z-score analysis to help you decide whether to use parametric or non-parametric tests for downstream analysis.

üêÅ HED (Human Equivalent Dose) Calculator

The HED Calculator converts drug doses between different animal species and humans based on Body Surface Area (BSA) normalization. This method is widely used in the initial stages of clinical trials to estimate the Maximum Safe Starting Dose (MRSD).

The calculation uses standard Km factors (Body Weight / Body Surface Area) for various species.

Reference (FDA Guidance):
Estimating the Maximum Safe Starting Dose in Initial Clinical Trials for Therapeutics in Adult Healthy Volunteers
U.S. Food and Drug Administration (July 2005)
