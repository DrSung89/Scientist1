User Guide & Detailed Information

üß™ Molarity Calculation

Molarity is the most common measure of concentration in biology and chemistry. It is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution.

Mass (g) = Concentration (mol/L) √ó Volume (L) √ó MW (g/mol)

üìä Outlier Detection: Why IQR?

Generally, outliers are detected using Z-scores (Standard Deviation), which assumes the data follows a normal distribution (Bell curve).

However, biological data is often skewed (asymmetric) and does not follow a perfect normal distribution. In these cases, using Z-scores can lead to errors. Therefore, we use the Interquartile Range (IQR) method, which is robust and reliable for non-normal distributions.

Calculation Formula:
1. Calculate IQR = Q3 (75th percentile) - Q1 (25th percentile)
2. Define Fences (Thresholds):
    ‚Ä¢ Lower Limit: Q1 - 1.5 √ó IQR
    ‚Ä¢ Upper Limit: Q3 + 1.5 √ó IQR

*Any data point outside these limits is considered an outlier.

This calculator converts doses between species based on Body Surface Area (BSA) normalization, not just body weight.

Planning Animal Studies from Human Doses:
When calculating a starting dose for animal experiments based on a human approved dose (e.g., a flat dose in mg), you must first convert it to mg/kg.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Note:
For this conversion, use the FDA standard human body weight of 60 kg. Do not use arbitrary weights unless specific data is available.

Step 1 (Human Dose to mg/kg):
Human Dose (mg/kg) = Total Human Dose (mg) / 60 kg

Step 2 (Conversion to Animal Dose):
Animal Dose (mg/kg) = Human Dose (mg/kg) √ó (Human Km / Animal Km)
