ğŸ“‰ What is Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis?

The Kaplan-Meier estimator is a non-parametric statistic used to estimate the survival function from lifetime data. In medical research, it is often used to measure the fraction of patients living for a certain amount of time after treatment.

Why "30.4375 days"?

When converting days to months in clinical trials, the SAS statistical software standard is widely used. Since a year has 365.25 days (accounting for leap years), the average length of a month is:

$$ 1 \text{ Month} = \frac{365.25}{12} = 30.4375 \text{ Days} $$

How to use this tool

  • Censoring (0): The subject left the study or the study ended before the event occurred. The exact survival time is unknown, but it is at least the recorded time.
  • Event (1): The event of interest (e.g., death, relapse) occurred at the recorded time.

Median Survival Time

This is the time at which the survival probability drops to 0.5 (50%). It is a key metric for comparing the efficacy of treatments.
