ğŸ“‰ How to Interpret the Plot

The Kaplan-Meier plot shows the probability of survival (or avoiding an event) over time.

  • The Y-Axis: Represents the survival probability (0.0 to 1.0, or 0% to 100%). It always starts at 1.0.
  • The Steps: Vertical drops indicate the occurrence of an event (1). The size of the drop depends on the number of subjects still at risk.
  • Flat Lines: Horizontal lines indicate periods where no events occurred.

â±ï¸ What is "Median Survival Time"?

This is a key statistic in medical research (e.g., oncology). It represents the time point at which 50% of the subjects have experienced the event (survival probability drops to 0.5).

  • "Not Reached": If the survival curve never drops below 0.5 (50%), the median survival time cannot be calculated. This usually means the treatment is very effective or the observation period was too short.
