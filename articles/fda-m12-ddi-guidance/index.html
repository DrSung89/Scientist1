Navigating the ICH M12 Guidance on Drug-Drug Interaction Studies

An analysis of the harmonized guidelines for evaluating pharmacokinetic interactions in drug development.

1. Introduction and Scope

The evaluation of drug-drug interactions (DDIs) is a critical component of pharmacological research. The International Council for Harmonisation (ICH) M12 guidance aims to harmonize regulatory expectations worldwide, effectively reducing duplicative clinical trials and standardizing safety reporting. The scope of the M12 guidance is primarily limited to pharmacokinetic interactions, focusing on metabolic enzyme- and transporter-mediated interactions for small chemical molecules.

2. In Vitro Evaluation of DDIs

Evaluating a drug's DDI potential usually begins with in vitro experiments to identify the principal routes of elimination. If oxidative metabolism is important, studies should first determine whether the drug is a substrate for the most common Cytochrome P450 (CYP) enzymes: CYP1A2, CYP2B6, CYP2C8, CYP2C9, CYP2C19, CYP2D6, and CYP3A.

If researchers need to perform accurate data analysis during these in vitro phases, utilizing tools like our Outlier Detector can help ensure the robustness of technical replicates.

3. Clinical Evaluation Strategies

Regulatory decision-making relies upon prospective studies specifically designed to evaluate DDI potential. The guidance defines several study types:

  • Stand-Alone DDI Studies: Conducted with the primary objective of determining the presence or absence of a clinical DDI and its magnitude.
  • Nested DDI Studies: Investigations evaluated as part of larger phase 2/3 clinical studies, where DDI assessment is not the primary objective.
  • Cocktail Approaches: Involves the simultaneous administration of substrates for multiple enzymes/transporters.

To reduce variability in the magnitude of DDIs during clinical evaluation, the guidance strictly recommends the exclusion of other medications, dietary/herbal supplements, tobacco, smoking, alcohol, and certain fruit juices.
