Navigating the ICH M12 Guidance on Drug-Drug Interaction Studies

A comprehensive review of the unified FDA/ICH M12 guidelines for in vitro and clinical evaluation of metabolic enzyme- and transporter-mediated DDIs.

1. Harmonizing Global DDI Standards

For years, pharmaceutical developers faced a fragmented regulatory landscape when assessing Drug-Drug Interactions (DDIs), navigating slightly differing guidances from the FDA, EMA, and PMDA. The adoption of the ICH M12 guideline represents a monumental harmonization effort, providing a single, globally accepted framework for evaluating enzyme- and transporter-mediated DDIs during drug development.

2. In Vitro Enzyme and Transporter Studies

The M12 guideline outlines specific cutoff values and methodologies for in vitro studies. Sponsors are mandated to evaluate a new investigational drug as both a victim (substrate) and a perpetrator (inhibitor/inducer) of major cytochrome P450 (CYP) enzymes, UGTs, and key drug transporters like P-gp, BCRP, OATP1B1, OAT1/3, and OCT2. The guidance enforces strict criteria for determining $IC_{50}$ and $K_i$ values, reducing false negatives in predicting clinical interactions.

3. Clinical Study Design and Risk Assessment

When an in vitro signal breaches the defined threshold, clinical DDI studies become mandatory. The M12 guidance provides specific recommendations for choosing appropriate index drugs (strong index inhibitors or inducers) and utilizing Physiologically Based Pharmacokinetic (PBPK) modeling. By standardizing these approaches, the ICH M12 guidance aims to generate more predictable safety profiles and precise prescribing information, ultimately protecting patients from fatal adverse drug events.
