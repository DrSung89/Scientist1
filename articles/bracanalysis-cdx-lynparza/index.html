BRACAnalysis CDx: Pioneering Companion Diagnostics for PARP Inhibitors

Reviewing the approval and mechanism of the first targeted diagnostic and therapeutic combination in ovarian cancer.

1. BRCA Mutations and Synthetic Lethality

Women with a deleterious BRCA mutation are predisposed to ovarian cancer due to deficient homologous recombination repair. Lynparza (olaparib) is a polyadenosine 5'diphosphoribose polymerase (PARP) inhibitor that targets these tumors through a process known as synthetic lethality. In this paradigm, neither the homologous recombination defect (HRD) nor PARP inhibition alone is cytotoxic, but the combination of the two leads to cell death as double-stranded DNA breaks accumulate.

2. The Role of BRACAnalysis CDx

Lynparza and its companion diagnostic test, BRACAnalysis, were approved by the US FDA in December 2014. Although many companion diagnostic kits are used in oncology (e.g., evaluating BRAF V600E), BRACAnalysis is unique. It comprehensively evaluates the BRCA1 and BRCA2 genes to assess for mutations across the entire gene rather than identifying a single point mutation. It utilizes PCR and Sanger sequencing to identify single nucleotide variants, and a multiplex PCR assay to detect large genomic rearrangements.

3. Clinical Validation and Impact

The approval was based largely on Study 19, a pivotal randomized Phase II trial for women with platinum-sensitive recurrent ovarian cancer. A bridging study was performed using archival samples to establish agreement between local BRCA test results and the BRACAnalysis CDx. The magnitude of clinical benefit in terms of progression-free survival (PFS) was highly significant for patients harboring germline BRCA mutations.
